Theoretically, the digital citizenship unit is the most important unit to cover in BBT. Why? Because the Internet is everywhere; we all use it, and thus we should know how to use it properly. I do reserve, however, that the (provincially-proscribed) structure of the unit was ineffective in serving this purpose, however that is no fault of the teacher. Some units of note include:
  • Unit 1: Digital Security
    • On a platform where it's very simple to hand over all your personal information and destroy your computer in the process, learning the ins-and-outs of digital security is a must.
  • Unit 3: Digital Rights and Responsibilities
    • When you've got the world on your screen, it's important to know how to behave in a way that accomodates others.
  • Unit 4: Digital Etiquette
    • When behind a screen, it can feel like the people you're talking to aren't people at all; that's why it's necessary to learn proper etiquette.
  • Unit 5: Digital Wellness
    • Now that everyone is thoroughly addicted to digital technology, it's crucial to maintain physical and mental health.
  • Unit 8: Digital Law
    • Why not pirate that season of your favourite show, while working on a reference-less research paper? Because it's illegal. This unit talks about the law and teaches what can and can't be done on the WWW.
